Verbal violence refers to any form of communication that involves insults, threats, humiliation, put-downs or any other type of language that causes emotional or psychological harm to a person. This may include insults and put-downs, threats and blackmail, humiliation and ridicule, disqualifications and belittling, and offensive and discriminatory language. Verbal violence can have negative consequences on people's mental and emotional health, such as anxiety and stress, depression and low self-esteem, sleep problems and fatigue, difficulties concentrating and making decisions, and feelings of guilt and shame. It is important to recognize that verbal violence is a form of abuse and is not acceptable in any relationship, and to seek help and support if you are experiencing verbal violence. Verbal violence can be prevented by practicing assertive and respectful communication, setting clear boundaries and respecting the boundaries of others, avoiding offensive and discriminatory language, actively listening to and trying to understand the perspectives of others, and seeking help and support if you are experiencing verbal violence.
